Logo Directions

Five distinct approaches — from evolved butterfly to fresh new marks. Pick which directions feel right, and we'll refine from there.

Full Logo
Icon

A. Refined Butterfly

The current butterfly, redrawn as clean vector art. Keeps the brand recognition Mom has built, but sharper and more consistent. Every size stays crisp.

Full Logo
Icon

B. Abstract Wings

Two gentle curves evoking butterfly wings — or open arms in an embrace. Simple enough to work at any size, from favicon to billboard. The two-tone treatment could reflect any palette.

Full Logo
Icon

C. CwG Monogram

A clean lettermark with a tiny butterfly accent. Works beautifully as a social media avatar, app icon, or email signature stamp. The full name sits alongside for context.

Full Logo
Icon

D. Typographic

Let the name itself be the mark, using beautiful typography as the primary design element. A tiny butterfly perches on the "G" as a subtle brand signature. Minimal, elegant, memorable.

Full Logo
Icon

E. Heart & Wing

A heart with graceful wing-like flourishes — combining "caring" (heart) with "grace" (the lift of a wing). Completely fresh while honoring the brand's meaning.

What We Need From You

This is an exploration, not a final product. We want your reactions.

  1. Color palettes: Which 1-2 palettes feel right for Caring with Grace? Any that feel completely wrong?
  2. Fonts: Which pairing feels most "us"? Remember these need to work in email signatures too.
  3. Logo directions: Which 1-2 approaches should we develop further? Fine to mix ideas (e.g., "I like the butterfly from A but the layout of D").
  4. Overall mood: Should the brand feel more classic/traditional or modern/fresh?

Don't worry about getting it perfect — this round is about narrowing directions. We'll refine your top picks in the next round.
